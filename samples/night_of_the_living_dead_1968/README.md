## About the Clips

20 WAV files (16 kHz, mono, 16-bit PCM) ranging from 5 to 120 seconds, extracted from different scenes featuring various speakers and contexts: dialogue between survivors, radio/TV news broadcasts, arguments, and tense exchanges. Leading silence has been trimmed from each clip.

File naming: `{duration}s_{first_few_words}.wav`

## Public Domain Status

Night of the Living Dead entered the public domain upon its release in 1968. The original distributor, the Walter Reade Organization, failed to place a copyright notice on the prints, which was required for copyright protection under the Copyright Act of 1909 then in effect. The film has been freely distributed, copied, and reused since its release.
